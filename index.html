Idea 01

1,32,000 people. One garba.

Aerial · A packed stadium garba at night

Every brand in India does Navratri. Only one franchise on earth can hold garba inside the world's largest stadium. The biggest garba ever danced, a world record on the line, the anthem remixed, players on stage. One evening, and the off-season question is answered forever.

Idea 02

Post through the loss.

Wins cement your success. Losses meld communities. RCB waited seventeen years for a trophy and built the league's biggest fandom while waiting.

GT has lost two finals and never said a word. So the jokes get made by everyone else. GT makes the joke first, the morning after, and the campaign answers it: Gujarat Tightens. Tighten your laces. Tighten your seatbelts. Tighten the field. The loss is the setup. The comeback is the brand.

Idea 03

Give the team a cast.

The Admin

A mascot made of posts

One unnamed Amdavadi runs the account, and everyone knows it. Jokes with rival admins. Gets scolded by management, on the record. Vanishes after a loss, returns with thepla. Duolingo built a global brand this way. No cricket team has dared.

Baby aur Ba

Two ages. One love. Cricket.

Ba reads omens and trusts no left-hander. Baby is eight and knows every stat. They disagree about everything, and the disagreement is the show. Prediction before every match, verdict the morning after, a special on auction day. One rule: a real Ba, found in Ahmedabad, never an actress.

Idea 04

Gujarat's favourites, in GT colours.

Gokuldham x GT

A tie-up with TMKOC

The most loved Gujarati family on television meets the state's team. Cast at the stadium, players visiting Gokuldham, matchday specials. When GT wins, all of Gokuldham celebrates, and half of India watches it happen.

The Thali Test

Every new signing faces the board

Fafda-jalebi. Undhiyu. Khichdi-kadhi. One surprise from Ba. Rabada meets ganthiya on camera. A welcome ritual, a food series, and instant proof the team belongs to the state it is named after.

Idea 06

The city becomes the feed.

GT Gully Gang

GGG · From the gully to the NaMo Stadium

Tennis-ball cricket in every gully of Gujarat, all summer. The winners walk into the Narendra Modi Stadium and play the final under its lights, on the Titans' own ground. The world's smallest format on the world's biggest stage. A local hero every week, and months of stories in between.

Rickshaw Shayari

Fans write. The city publishes.

Rickshaw-back poetry is a living Ahmedabad tradition. Fans send their best GT lines, the winning two hundred get painted across the city's rickshaws with the fan's name signed under each. People photograph them in traffic. The city does the marketing.

Idea 07

Merch from sacred to stupid.

The sacred end

A mascot, a mythology, merch of GT's own

The Titan finally gets a face, and a folk-art rendering in Mata ni Pachedi and Pithora styles behind it. Lore no team can copy, unmistakably Gujarati at first glance.

The stupid end

Announced deadpan. Gone in hours.

Official GT chhaas for the dry state. The thepla tin. A nap pillow for the sacred Gujarati afternoon. Limited drops, screenshot by every meme page in the country. The merch is the joke, and the joke is the media plan.

Idea 08

The match, with the volume up.

The Alt Stream

Two comedians. Zero neutrality.

An official second commentary for home matches, in Gujarati, openly on GT's side. Young fans already watch with memes on a second phone. GT makes that second screen its own.

The Locker Room Feed

Players on Gujarati meme audio

What Juventus players did with Italian meme music, GT players do with ours: Khalasi, the audio of the week, grainy hard-images with Gujarati captions. The content that makes cricketers feel like boys from here.
